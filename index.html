  - Steps to Change or Cancel a Flight Schedule Completely

  1. The first step is to contact the airline's customer service or the ticket booking agent where you purchased your ticket. Make sure you have your reservation number and other ticket details to facilitate a faster and smoother process. You can contact them via phone, email, or online chat services available.

  2. Once connected, give your full details such as passenger name, reservation number, and departure date. Clearly state that you want to change your flight schedule or cancel the ticket. If you want to reschedule, specify the new date and time you prefer.

  3. After submitting your request, follow the procedures provided by the airline or agent. Usually, there will be additional fees or administrative charges depending on the airline's policies. Be sure to understand the terms and conditions, and be prepared to pay any necessary fees.

  4. Once the process is complete, ask for a written confirmation via email or other official proof that the schedule change or cancellation has been processed. Keep this proof as documentation in case you need it later.

  5. If you cancel your ticket, inquire about the refund process and how long it will take for the funds to be returned to your account. If there are any credits or vouchers from the airline, note the usage terms for future travel.

  - Five Mandatory Rules for Passengers During Flight

Passengers must follow all instructions and directions from the flight crew during the flight, including during takeoff, in-flight, and landing. This is important for safety and smooth operation.

Passengers are required to wear their seatbelts when the seatbelt sign is on, especially during takeoff, landing, and turbulence. Make sure the seatbelt is fastened properly.

Passengers should keep the cabin clean and avoid littering. Additionally, avoid disruptive behavior such as making loud noises, talking loudly, or acting disrespectfully to others.

Smoking is strictly prohibited throughout the aircraft, including in the toilets. The use of fire or open flames is also forbidden to ensure safety during the flight.

Passengers must turn off or set their electronic devices to airplane mode as instructed, especially during takeoff and landing. Using devices that interfere with the aircraftâ€™s systems should be avoided.
